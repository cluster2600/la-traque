# LA TRAQUE DE L'ATTENTAT DU MÉTRO 14
## CHAPTER 6: SURVIVORS

The hospital is Hôpital Lariboisière. 8:15 AM. Thomas has been there for two hours. The survivors are scattered across three floors. Some in ICU. Some in regular wards. Some already discharged. Thomas starts with the ones who can talk.

The first is a woman. Age sixty-three. Name is Marguerite Leclerc. She was sitting three seats from the blast. Her left arm is bandaged. Broken radius. She's alert. Rational. She remembers everything.

"Where were you sitting?" Thomas asks. Simple question. Most important.

"By the window. The left side of the car. Third seat from the rear door."

"Did you see anyone place anything under the seats?"

"No. I didn't see anything. But I wasn't paying attention. I was reading."

Thomas makes a note. "Before the explosion. Did you notice anything unusual? Anyone acting strangely? Anyone getting off just before the doors closed?"

Marguerite thinks. "There was a man. He was nervous. Very nervous. He got on at Châtelet. He kept looking at his watch. Then he got off at the next stop. Very quick. Very deliberate."

"Which direction was he sitting?"

"In front of me. A few seats forward. I remember because he was so anxious. You notice anxiety. You know?"

Thomas nods. "What did he look like?"

"North African. Maybe forty years old. Dark hair. Wearing a blue jacket. That's all I remember."

Thomas writes it down. Malik Benhadj is forty-one. Blue jacket. Anxiety.

He moves to the next survivor. A young man. Age twenty-two. Broken leg. Concussion. Name is Antoine Deschamps.

"I was standing," Antoine says. "Holding the pole. Right side of the car. Middle section."

"Did you see anyone suspicious?"

"Everyone's suspicious when something explodes," Antoine says. The humor is how he copes. "But no. I didn't see anything before."

"After? When you were trapped? Did you hear anything? Anyone talking? Any indication of how many people were alive?"

Antoine closes his eyes. Reliving it.

"There was a lot of screaming. Then silence. Then crying. Children crying. An old man was trying to keep people calm. He kept saying 'the rescue will come. The rescue will come.' I think there were about forty people. Maybe more. Some weren't moving. I don't know if they were unconscious or dead."

"Could you hear anyone moving around? Anyone walking? Anyone doing anything that seemed deliberate?"

"No. Everyone was just... trapped. Waiting. Most people were too shocked to move."

Thomas leaves the hospital at 11:45 AM. He has six interviews. Six pieces of information. One clear piece of intelligence: a man in a blue jacket got on at Châtelet and got off at the next stop. Got off just before the explosion.

That's not an accident. That's a timer.

Back at DGSE headquarters. Thomas calls Malik Benhadj into the interrogation room. Not an arrest. Not yet. An interview. Casual. Routine.

Malik is sitting. He's calm. He's educated. He's polite. He's also a terrorist.

"We're interviewing all passengers from the morning commute," Thomas says. "Routine. Just checking details."

"Of course," Malik says. "I'm happy to help. But I don't understand. I got off at the next stop. I wasn't in the middle car."

"No," Thomas says. "But you were in the first car. And you got off just before something happened."

"That's coincidence."

"Was it?" Thomas leans forward. Not aggressive. Just present. "Because we have eyewitness testimony. Someone saw you. Nervous. Watching your watch. Getting off very deliberately."

Malik's face doesn't change. But his pupils dilate. Just slightly. Just enough.

"I have anxiety," Malik says. "I'm often nervous."

"On the day of a bombing? You're nervous about something else? Something so important that you can't wait?"

"I had a doctor's appointment."

Thomas doesn't believe him. But he doesn't push. Not yet.

"We'll verify that," Thomas says. "With your doctor."

He lets Malik leave. Not because he's innocent. But because Thomas needs to know more. Malik is part of the cell. But he's not the leader. Yasmine is the leader. Thomas needs to find Yasmine.

Back at the office. Moreau is waiting.

"We found something," Moreau says. "Financial records show a wire transfer. Three thousand euros. Sent to a safe house in the 11th arrondissement. The transfer came from an account registered to a company called Soleil Méditerranéen. Limited liability. Registered in Algeria but operating in Paris."

"Who owns it?"

"We're working on that. But preliminary investigation shows the company is a front. It imports olive oil. Or supposedly does. The amounts don't match any real import records. It's money laundering."

Thomas nods. "Get me the address of the safe house. And get me everyone associated with Soleil Méditerranéen. Now."

The safe house is in the 11th. Rue Saint-Ambroise. Fourth floor. Apartment 7. Thomas gets there at 2:30 PM with a tactical team.

The door is unlocked. Nobody's home. But the apartment is lived in. Recently.

In the kitchen, there's evidence: blueprints of the Métro Line 14. Maps with station locations marked. Notes in Arabic. A list of names. Hassan. Inès. Malik. Yasmine.

And a timer. On the counter. Broken. The same model as the third device.

Thomas picks it up carefully. This is the evidence. This is the proof.

He calls the lab. "Send a team. And get me everything you can on this timer. Manufacture date. Where it was purchased. Who might have bought it. I want to know everything."

The blueprints are photographed. The apartment is sealed. A team will process it for fingerprints. For DNA. For anything that connects to the cell.

Thomas stands in the kitchen and looks at the maps. The marks on the stations. This is planning. This is serious. This is not improvisation.

Yasmine didn't just wake up one morning and decide to bomb the Métro. This was planned. For weeks. Maybe months.

The timer is broken. Just like the third device. This is important. Thomas thinks about this.

Was it sabotage? Was someone in the cell trying to prevent the third bomb from detonating? Or was it just mechanical failure?

He makes a note: "Investigate timer failure. Possible internal conflict within cell."

By 6:00 PM, Thomas has three pieces of intelligence:

One: Malik Benhadj was in the first car. He got off just before the explosion. He was watching his watch. He was nervous. He was activating the timer.

Two: The safe house contains blueprints, maps, evidence of planning. Names of all four cell members.

Three: The third timer failed. Either by accident or by deliberate sabotage.

Thomas is getting closer. But Yasmine is still free. Hassan is still free. Inès is still free.

They're planning the next attack. The clock is running. Thomas can feel it.
